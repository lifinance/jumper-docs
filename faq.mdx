## FAQ

### What is Jumper?

Jumper is a consumer exchange that lets you swap and bridge across many chains from one interface.

### Do I need an account?

No account required. Connect a wallet. Some fiat on-ramp providers may require identity checks.

### Which chains and tokens are supported?

Support changes over time. Jumper shows what is currently available for the selected chains.

### Why do I need to approve a token?

Approvals let a contract spend a token for swapping. Many ERC20 tokens require approval before swapping.

### Can I cancel a transaction?

Once submitted, you generally cannot cancel. Some wallets let you replace a pending transaction with a higher-gas one.

### How long does a bridge take?

Depends on route and network conditions. Some complete quickly; others take longer during congestion.

### What is Gas Top Up?

It adds a small amount of the native gas token on the destination chain so you can transact after bridging.

### Why did my transaction fail?

Common reasons: not enough gas, slippage too low, route liquidity changed, or temporary provider issues. Try a smaller amount or a different token pair.

### Are there limits?

Some providers have minimums/maximums. Fiat on-ramps may enforce limits by region and verification level.

### Is Jumper powered by LI.FI?

Jumper uses LI.FI infrastructure for routing and execution. You do not need to understand LI.FI to use Jumper.

## Glossary

- **Chain:** A blockchain network such as Ethereum, Arbitrum, Base, or Solana.
- **Token:** A crypto asset such as ETH, USDC, or DAI.
- **Swap:** Trading one token for another on the same chain.
- **Bridge:** Moving value from one chain to another.
- **Route:** The set of steps used to complete your swap or bridge.
- **Gas:** The network fee required to submit a transaction.
- **Slippage:** Allowed price movement between estimate and final execution.
- **Explorer:** A site that shows onchain transaction details.
