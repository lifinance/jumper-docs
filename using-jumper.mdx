Welcome to Jumper. Jumper lets you swap and bridge tokens across many chains from one interface.

## Getting started

### What you need

1. A wallet (MetaMask, Rabby, Coinbase Wallet, WalletConnect compatible)
2. Some crypto in your wallet (e.g., USDC, ETH) on any supported chain
3. A little gas on your starting chain to submit transactions

### First-time checklist

1. Connect your wallet
2. Pick the From chain
3. Pick the From token
4. Pick the To chain
5. Pick the To token
6. Enter an amount
7. Review the route summary
8. Confirm in your wallet

## Core ideas

### Swap vs bridge

- A swap changes one token into another on the same chain.
- A bridge moves value from one chain to another.
- Jumper can combine both: e.g., start with USDC on Arbitrum and end with ETH on Base.

### Routes

A route is the plan Jumper uses to complete your request. It can include one or more steps. You always see a preview before confirming.

### Slippage

Slippage is the difference between the estimate and the final output. Lower slippage fails more often; higher slippage fills more often but can worsen price.

### Gas

- You pay gas on the chain where you sign.
- Multi-step routes can require gas for more than one transaction.

## Connect your wallet

1. Open Jumper.
2. Click **Connect wallet**.
3. Choose your wallet provider.
4. Approve the connection in your wallet.

<Tip>Use the right browser profile and wallet account. If the site shows the wrong account, disconnect and reconnect.</Tip>

## Swap (same chain)

Use Swap to trade tokens on a single chain.

<Steps>
<Step title="Select inputs">
- From chain and From token
- To token on the same chain
</Step>
<Step title="Enter amount">
Review the live estimate.
</Step>
<Step title="Confirm">
Click **Swap**, confirm any approval if prompted, then sign the swap.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Note>Some tokens require an approval first. Approval is a separate transaction.</Note>

## Bridge (cross-chain)

Use Bridge to move tokens to another chain.

<Steps>
<Step title="Select route details">
- From chain and token
- To chain and token
</Step>
<Step title="Review estimate">
Check output, time, and fees.
</Step>
<Step title="Confirm and track">
Click **Bridge** and confirm in your wallet. Track progress in the UI.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Info>Bridges can take seconds to minutes, longer if networks or providers are congested. Some routes include a swap before or after the bridge.</Info>

## Swap and bridge in one flow

This is the most common Jumper flow.

Example: Start with USDC on Arbitrum and receive ETH on Base in one combined route. Jumper handles the swap + bridge sequence for you.

## Gas Top Up

If you land on a new chain with no native gas token, Gas Top Up adds a small amount so you can transact.

- Use when bridging to a new chain or when you need gas for the next action.
- Start small; add more if you plan multiple actions.

## Fiat on ramp

Buy crypto with traditional payment methods, then continue onchain.

<Steps>
<Step title="Choose target">Pick the chain and token you want.</Step>
<Step title="Select provider">Pick an on-ramp provider and complete any checks.</Step>
<Step title="Pay and receive">Complete payment and receive crypto in your wallet.</Step>
</Steps>

Availability and processing time depend on your region and payment method.

## Rewards, loyalty, and missions

Jumper may offer missions and loyalty rewards. Common patterns:

- Complete a swap or bridge to earn points
- Use a featured partner chain or app
- Reach volume milestones over a time window

Rules vary by campaign—always read mission details.

## Fees and pricing

- **Network gas fees:** paid to the blockchain, not to Jumper.
- **Provider fees:** may apply depending on the chosen route.
- **Price impact and slippage:** thin liquidity can lower output.

Review screen shows estimated output, time, and known fees (plus expected gas).

## Transaction status and tracking

After you confirm, track progress in the Jumper UI.

Common statuses: waiting for wallet confirmation → pending on source → bridging in progress → completed. Multi-step routes may require more than one transaction.

## Safety and best practices

- Double check From/To chains and tokens.
- Be careful with approvals—prefer limited approvals when possible.
- Watch for fake sites; bookmark the official Jumper domain.
- Use small test transfers on new chains or tokens.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="My transaction is stuck pending">
    1) Check the transaction in your wallet. 2) Open the explorer link to confirm it is still pending. 3) If gas is too low, try speeding up in your wallet.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="My bridge is taking too long">
    Congestion or extra verification can slow bridges. Keep the tracking page open and avoid repeating the transfer unless the UI shows failure.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Token not found">
    The token may not exist on that chain. Switch to the correct chain or pick another token.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I bridged but cannot act on the destination chain">
    You likely have no gas. Use Gas Top Up or receive a small native token amount from another wallet.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Output changed after confirm">
    Price movement or slippage limits can change output. Try a lower amount, a different token pair, or adjust slippage if you accept the tradeoff.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I do not see my tokens">
    1) Confirm status is completed. 2) Switch to the correct destination chain in your wallet. 3) Add the token to your wallet if it is not shown by default.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
